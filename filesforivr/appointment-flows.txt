  Marie INC · powered by the AI assistant + Amela Bell's Google Calendar

------------------------------------------------------------------
  FLOW 1 — Marie books an appointment (call / voicemail)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Marie calls the Telnyx business number
      │
      ▼
IVR greeting plays; Marie leaves a voicemail
("Hi, I'd like to book an appointment...")
      │
      ▼
Voicemail is recorded and transcribed
      │
      ▼
AI analyzes the message
(ignores the "welcome to..." greeting; what Marie SAYS
 wins over whichever key she pressed)
      │
      ▼
Intent detected  →  "Booking / appointment request"
      │
      ▼
Is Amela Bell's Google Calendar connected?
      │
      ├── YES  →  send a booking link
      │        │
      │        ▼
      │   AI texts Marie:
      │   "Happy to help! Book a time that works for you: <link>"
      │   (if Marie named a time, the link opens pre-selected on it,
      │    with her name + number already filled in)
      │        │
      │        ▼
      │   Marie opens the link  →  sees Amela's REAL open times
      │   (business hours MINUS whatever is already busy on her calendar)
      │        │
      │        ▼
      │   Marie picks an open slot  →  confirms
      │        │
      │        ▼
      │   Event is created on Amela's Google Calendar
      │   (with a Google Meet link, tagged with Marie's phone)
      │        │
      │        ▼
      │   Booked on BOTH sides · Marie gets a confirmation
      │   · she can cancel or reschedule anytime
      │
      └── NO  →  no calendar connected
               │
               ▼
          AI texts Marie: "What day and time works best for you?"
               │
               ▼
          Marie replies with a time
               │
               ▼
          A reply is drafted and queued for the team to confirm


------------------------------------------------------------------
  FLOW 2 — How availability is decided (inside the booking link)
------------------------------------------------------------------

Marie opens the booking link
      │
      ▼
Read Amela Bell's business hours (e.g. Mon–Fri 10am–6pm)
      │
      ▼
Read Amela's calendar free/busy for the next 2 weeks
      │
      ▼
Open slots  =  business hours  MINUS  anything already booked
      │
      ▼
Show ONLY those open slots to Marie
      │
      ├── Marie picks one  →  it is booked; that slot disappears
      │                        (no double-booking)
      │
      └── The exact time Marie asked for is taken/closed
               ▼
          "That time isn't open — here are other times that day"


------------------------------------------------------------------
  FLOW 3 — Marie asks about an existing appointment
------------------------------------------------------------------

Marie calls or texts:
"When was my last appointment?"  /  "Anything booked for Friday?"
      │
      ▼
AI detects an appointment-history question
      │
      ▼
Look up Marie's appointments on Amela's calendar
(matched by PHONE NUMBER — reliable — then email, then name)
      │
      ├── Found
      │      ▼
      │   AI replies with the real date/time:
      │   "Your last appointment was Saturday, June 20 at 2pm."
      │   "Yes — you're booked Friday, July 4 at 1pm."
      │
      └── None on file
             ▼
          "I don't see any appointments on file for you —
           would you like to book one?"


------------------------------------------------------------------
  KEY POINTS FOR THE CLIENT
------------------------------------------------------------------
· Amela connects Google Calendar ONCE (one click). No links to paste.
· The customer always picks from Amela's REAL availability — never a
  time that's already busy or outside her hours.
· Every booking lands on Amela's calendar with a Meet link, and the
  customer can cancel/reschedule themselves.
· Appointments are matched by phone number, so history lookups stay
  accurate even if the customer gives a different name.
